Have you recently been diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease?

What's the next step?

This website provides information for patients that have been newly diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease. Although contacts are mainly available for people living in Oxfordshire and South Northamptonshire, many useful tips and information are accessible for everyone.

You will find explanations on symptoms, medicine management, details of the care Pathway for patients within the Oxford University Hospitals Trust and contact details for the local Parkinson's Disease Nurses.

About this Website

What is Parkinson?

Parkinson's Disease affects the neurons in the brain responsible for the release of dopamine, which is a chemical neuro-transmitter that helps your body move. Dopamine sends messages to the body to help control movements, but this action is affected when you have Parkinson.


It is very important to remember that Parkinson's disease affects people in many different ways, therefore your symptoms and treatment may be different from one person to another.
