What is Parkinson?

Parkinson's Disease affects the neurons in the brain responsible for the release of dopamine, which is a chemical neuro-transmitter that helps your body move. Dopamine sends messages to the body to help control movements, but this action is affected when you have Parkinson.


It is very important to remember that Parkinson's disease affects people in many different ways, therefore your symptoms and treatment may be different from one person to another.
